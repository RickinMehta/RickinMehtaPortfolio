Academic · St. Xavier’s
↓ Read Paper
The Problem
Analyse India’s most consequential post-liberalisation corporate conflict — going beyond the personality clash narrative to the structural root.
My Approach
Applied Fisher and Ury’s BATNA framework to the Tata Sons AoA amendments of 2012. Cross-referenced NCLT, NCLAT, Supreme Court, and IMD governance analysis.
↗ Central argument: 2012 AoA gave Trusts a permanently superior BATNA, making Mistry’s removal a structural inevitability. Absence of any dispute mechanism was the definitive governance failure.

02 — About

Who I am and what I’m
building toward

I’m a first-year student figuring out what I’m good at by actually doing things. So far that’s involved fintech, equity markets, case competitions, and a lot of cold outreach.

I’m doing a dual degree across St. Xavier’s College, Mumbai (BMS) and IIM Bangalore (BBA) at the same time. It’s a lot, but it keeps things interesting.

Outside of coursework, I spend a lot of time reading — mostly about geopolitics, philosophy, and how businesses actually get built. I find that the same questions that come up in Thucydides or Machiavelli show up in how companies compete and how markets move. That cross-pollination is something I actively think about.

I’m drawn to entrepreneurship not just as a career path but as a way of thinking — identifying gaps, testing ideas, and building things from scratch. Most of my work so far has been self-initiated for exactly that reason.

Longer term I’m interested in finance, consulting, and eventually building something of my own. Keeping options open while making sure every year adds something real.

04 — Thoughts

Corporate Strategy · Featured Essay
Why the Tata–Mistry Conflict Was Decided in 2012, Not 2016
The dismissal is framed as a boardroom coup. My reading: a structural inevitability the moment the AoA gave Tata Trusts veto power while handing Mistry the title without the authority. When one party’s BATNA is permanently stronger, negotiated coexistence is always temporary.
Geopolitics
Why Every Business Student Should Understand Geopolitics
Supply chains, capital flows, and market access are all downstream of political geography. The companies that got blindsided by recent disruptions mostly ignored this. A case for why geopolitical literacy belongs in the business toolkit.
Philosophy · Strategy
What Machiavelli Actually Got Right About Organisations
Strip away the reputation and The Prince is a fairly sharp analysis of power, legitimacy, and institutional change. More relevant to corporate governance than most strategy textbooks.
Entrepreneurship
The Difference Between Starting a Business and Entrepreneurial Thinking
Most people conflate the two. Entrepreneurial thinking — identifying gaps, testing assumptions, acting under uncertainty — is useful everywhere. It’s a mode of operating, not just a career path.
Brand Strategy
Community Is a Moat. Most Brands Are Renting It.
Why Rush Nutrition’s real advantage is owning the physical space where fitness and social overlap. A framework for D2C brands building offline flywheel models in India.
Finance
What Managing a Live Portfolio Taught Me About Conviction
Holding ACC through +16.67% required a thesis, not a feeling. On the difference between research-based confidence and noise-driven trading.
